Special items:

Umbrella: An umbrella to keep you dry on a rainy day.

Sunscreen: Avoid sunburns and skin damage by applying sunscreen when you are outside.

Snowpants: Stay warm on a cold day. This icon also refers to an underlayer (e.g. long johns)

First layer:

Dress: A one-piece dress.

T-shirt: This mainly describes a regular t-shirt, with the option of an underlayer (either under or as the t-shirt).

Long sleeve: A t-shirt with long sleeves. Not to be confused with a thin hoodie/sweater.

Pants:

Shorts/Skirt: The shortest form of pants and usually worn on hotter days.

Long pants: Mainly describes pants such as jeans and sweatpants. They are on the thicker side.

Breezy/Athletic pants: Compared to long pants, these are thinner and can be worn on warmer days.

Outer layer:

Thin sweater: This describes any piece of clothing you would wear overtop your first layer. However, this item is on the thinner side.

Thick sweater: This is similar to a thin sweater, except for the fact that it is thicker.

Windbreaker: This falls between thick and thin sweater. It also may be preferable on rainy days. This is originally not paired with a winter coat.

Winter Coat: This describes your thick winter coat. This is the only piece of clothing that can be worn with other outer layers, except for the windbreaker (You can end up training this yourself, but this may take longer to train)
